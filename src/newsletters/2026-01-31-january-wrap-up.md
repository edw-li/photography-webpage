## January Challenge: Urban Geometry

What a month! We received **34 submissions** from 18 members for our first challenge of the year. The theme *Urban Geometry* pushed everyone to look at the city through fresh eyes, finding lines, patterns, and shapes hiding in plain sight.

### Winner: "Convergence" by Priya Sharma

Priya's stunning capture of the downtown transit hub at dusk took the top spot. The converging lines of the railway tracks, combined with the geometric glass ceiling reflecting the sunset, created an image that perfectly embodied the theme.

**Priya says:** "I'd walked through that station a hundred times without really seeing it. This challenge made me stop and look up for once."

### Runner-Up: "Grid" by Jamal Washington

Jamal's abstract close-up of a parking garage facade turned mundane concrete into a mesmerizing pattern study. The interplay of shadow and light across the repeating rectangular openings gave the image an almost Mondrian-like quality.

### Honorable Mentions

- **"Spiral"** by Marco Bellini — A drone shot looking straight down into a spiral parking ramp
- **"Crosswalk Shadows"** by Taylor Brooks — Pedestrians casting long geometric shadows across a zebra crossing
- **"The Cube"** by Casey Kim — A macro shot of a reflective glass building corner at blue hour
