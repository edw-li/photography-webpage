## From Shy Beginner to Street Photography Leader

When Jordan Lee first walked into a Bridgeway meetup three years ago, they'd never shown their photography to anyone. Today, Jordan serves as our Vice President and has had street photography featured in two regional exhibitions.

### The Turning Point

"I remember my first photo critique session," Jordan recalls. "I was terrified to show my work. But the feedback was so constructive and encouraging that I left wanting to shoot more, not less. That's what Bridgeway does — it makes you braver."

### Finding a Voice in Street Photography

Jordan's style has evolved into a distinctive blend of candid moments and architectural framing. Their signature technique — shooting through glass reflections to layer multiple narratives in a single frame — has become instantly recognizable.

### Advice for New Members

Jordan offers three pieces of advice for anyone just starting out:

1. **Shoot every day**, even if it's just with your phone
2. **Share your work** — the critique circle is the fastest way to improve
3. **Try a genre you think you'd hate** — Jordan originally planned to shoot landscapes

### Recent Achievement

Jordan's series *"Commuters"* was selected for the Regional Arts Council's annual photography showcase this March. The series documents the quiet, unguarded moments of people on public transit — a project that started as a monthly challenge submission right here at Bridgeway.

Congratulations, Jordan! We're all cheering you on.
